Expert Systems

Introduction

Expert Systems are computer programs that simulate human expertise to solve complex problems in specific domains. They use a knowledge base and an inference engine to analyze data and provide recommendations.

Components of Expert Systems

Knowledge Base

Stores domain-specific knowledge, including facts and heuristics.

Inference Engine

Processes knowledge and applies reasoning to reach conclusions.

User Interface

Allows users to interact with the system to input data and receive responses.

Applications

  • Healthcare: Medical diagnosis and treatment recommendations.
  • Finance: Risk analysis and fraud detection.
  • Engineering: Fault detection and maintenance prediction.
  • Education: Intelligent tutoring systems.

Challenges

  • Knowledge acquisition and maintenance.
  • Limited adaptability to new situations.
  • Potential bias in decision-making.

Future of Expert Systems

Advancements in AI and machine learning are enhancing expert systems, making them more adaptable, accurate, and intelligent.
